Who Are The Sama–Badjao?

The Sama–Badjao, or “Sea Nomads,” are an Indigenous maritime people from the Sulu Archipelago. Traditionally living on houseboats and moving across coastal waters, they are known for their seafaring skills, boat-building, weaving, music, and rituals tied to the ocean. Even as some now live in stilt houses along the shore, their culture and identity remain deeply connected to the sea.
